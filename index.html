Frequently asked questions

How does AI Query Revealer work technically?

The extension injects a script into AI platform pages (ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude) that intercepts the fetch/XHR requests in real time. When AI runs a web search to answer your question, we capture the actual search queries, the sources returned, and which ones get cited in the response. Everything happens locally in your browser — no data is sent to any external server.

Is my data private? What do you collect?

Your data never leaves your browser. All interception, analysis, and storage happens locally via Chrome's storage API. We don't have servers collecting your queries or browsing data. The only external calls are for license verification (encrypted key check) — your actual AI usage data stays 100% on your machine.

Which AI platforms are supported?

Currently, full support for ChatGPT (queries + cited + consulted sources), Claude (queries + sources), and Gemini (queries + partial sources). Perplexity and Grok support are in development. We ship platform updates regularly as AI providers change their formats.

What's the difference between "cited" and "consulted" sources?

When AI searches the web, it fetches dozens of pages (consulted sources). But it only references a fraction of them in its answer (cited sources). This distinction is crucial for SEO — understanding why some sources get cited while others are read and discarded helps you optimize your content for citation.

What is the GEO Score?

GEO stands for Generative Engine Optimization. Your GEO Score (0-100) measures how visible your website is in AI-generated answers. It combines 6 dimensions: citation visibility, platform coverage, source diversity, competitive position, trend direction, and consistency. It also includes a technical scan of your site (robots.txt, schema markup, E-E-A-T signals, page speed).

How is this different from tools like Zicy or Otterly?

Those tools run prompts on your behalf and track whether your brand appears in the results. AI Query Revealer takes a fundamentally different approach: we intercept the actual hidden searches that AI runs while you use it naturally. What is Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)?

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the emerging practice of optimizing your website and content to appear as a cited source in AI-generated answers. As platforms like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude increasingly answer user questions by searching the web behind the scenes, a new frontier of search visibility has emerged — one that traditional SEO tools don't address.

Why GEO matters for SEO professionals

Research from Princeton and Georgia Tech shows that AI sessions have grown by 527% in 2025, and 63% of websites that receive AI traffic also see improvements in their traditional search rankings. Being cited in AI answers is no longer optional — it's becoming a core part of content strategy. Unlike traditional SEO where you optimize for keywords and backlinks, GEO requires you to optimize for AI citation patterns: structured data, authoritative content, freshness signals, and technical accessibility.

The 6 pillars of AI visibility

AI Query Revealer measures your GEO Score across 6 dimensions that determine whether AI platforms cite your content:

1. AI Visibility — How often your domain appears as a cited source in AI answers, weighted by the total number of searches analyzed.
2. Multi-platform coverage — Whether you're cited on one or multiple AI platforms (ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude), indicating broad vs. narrow visibility.
3. Query diversity — The range of different query topics where your site appears, measuring topical authority across your niche.
4. Competitive position — Your ranking among cited sources. Being the first source cited carries more weight than appearing last.
5. Trend direction — Whether your citation rate is increasing, stable, or declining over recent sessions.
6. Consistency — How regularly your site appears across sessions, distinguishing reliable authority from one-time mentions.

How to improve your AI visibility

Based on analysis of thousands of AI searches intercepted by AI Query Revealer, here are the key factors that influence whether AI cites your content:

Schema markup — Sites with Article, Organization, FAQPage, and HowTo schemas are cited 2.4x more frequently by AI.
E-E-A-T signals — Author bios, datePublished, dateModified, and Person schema directly influence AI trust and citation rates.
robots.txt access — Allowing GPTBot, ClaudeBot, and Google-Extended ensures AI crawlers can read and index your content.
Content freshness — Pages updated within the last 90 days are significantly more likely to be cited. Maintain an active sitemap with recent dates.
Structured answers — Content formatted with clear headings, concise paragraphs, and direct answers to specific questions matches how AI selects source material.
Page performance — Fast load times and clean HTML structure improve both AI crawler access and citation probability.

The Reformulation Gap: what AI actually searches vs. what you asked

One of the most valuable insights from AI Query Revealer is the Reformulation Gap — the difference between what you type and what the AI actually searches for. When you ask "best CRM for small business", ChatGPT might search for "top rated CRM software SMB 2026 comparison review". Understanding this gap reveals which keywords AI considers authoritative and helps you align your content with AI's actual search behavior rather than assumed user intent.

GEO vs. traditional SEO: understanding the difference

Traditional SEO optimizes for search engine result pages (SERPs). GEO optimizes for being cited in AI-generated answers. The two are complementary: strong SEO helps AI find your content, and strong GEO ensures AI cites it. However, GEO introduces unique factors like the distinction between consulted and cited sources, multi-platform coverage, and the reformulation gap that have no direct equivalent in traditional SEO.

AI Query Revealer is the first tool that lets you observe, measure, and optimize for all of these factors from real AI usage data — not simulated queries.
